Abstract

In human motion prediction (HMP), the current progress mainly relies on real values to represent human motion and using real-valued networks for prediction. Despite substantial progress, they inherently suffer from manifold mismatch—failing to adequately model the quasi-periodic nature of human motion signals. This limitation leads to error accumulation, motion jitter, and amplitude attenuation in long-term forecasting. % This work aims breaks the existing paradigm, Complex-valued neural networks (CVNNs) offer a natural solution, as their algebraic properties align seamlessly with rotational dynamics and phase-coherent signals, a synergy proven effective in phase-sensitive domains (e.g., human motion). To unlock this potential for HMP, we propose a unified framework integrating two core innovations: Spherical Complex Decomposition (SCD) and an Amplitude-Preserving Complex Network. SCD decouples 3D bone vectors into fixed scalar lengths and directional components encoded as orthogonal complex numbers, enforcing skeletal rigidity as a hard geometric constraint and eliminating bone-stretching artifacts. The accompanying complex network abandons traditional coordinate regression, instead leveraging phase dynamics to model motion evolution—enhanced by a kinematic flow graph for spatial dependency modeling, periodic-aware phase attention for capturing temporal periodicity, and frequency domain enhancement via FFT for complex motion pattern adaptation.
